01

Executive Summary

The verdict

Sisterly is a fundamentally strong brand with a premium product, genuine LTV, and a loyal subscription base. The business is healthy underneath. The paid acquisition layer is not.

Across Meta and Google, Sisterly spent €118,985 in four months. On Google, 70% of attributed revenue came from branded search — people already looking for Sisterly by name. Strip that out and the incremental return on ad spend is deeply negative across both platforms. Meta is running at 1.63x blended ROAS and €20k is burning in Google Demand Gen at 0.45x ROAS.

The agency deserves credit for the email automation infrastructure and shows genuine capability in the IE Meta campaigns. But the overall fee of €12,600/month is not justified by the results. Revenue grew just 0.3% YoY against significantly higher spend.

AOV of €103 changes the margin picture. At 40–50% gross margin, first-purchase gross profit (€41–52) does not cover the ad-only CAC of €63. Sisterly is loss-making on new customer acquisition at first order unless margin exceeds ~60%. Newer customers are spending significantly less from the outset — suggesting lower AOV entry products, heavier discounting, or lower-intent traffic from broad paid targeting.

The 2.51x blended ROAS is almost entirely brand search. 70% of Google's attributed revenue (€72k of €103k) came from branded search — people already looking for Sisterly. This is captured intent, not generated demand. Strip brand out and the non-brand Google account is deeply loss-making.
Demand Gen is the single biggest waste in the audit. Nearly 50% of the Google budget (€20,264) is going to Demand Gen campaigns running at 0.45x ROAS. €17,273 in prospecting Demand Gen returned just €9,545 in conversion value. These are awareness-format placements being run on a direct response budget. They should be cut immediately.
Generic non-brand search is broken. The IRE generic search spent €4,661 at a €171 CPA. The UK women's search spent €1,889 at €315 CPA and 0.35x ROAS. Both need fundamental restructuring — keywords, match types, landing page alignment, or all three.
IRE Shopping is the hidden gem. Sisterlyed IRE shopping campaign achieved 7.73x ROAS at a €17 CPA — the best in the entire Google account — yet received just €1,418 in spend. Scaling this should be an immediate priority.
UK search is stalled. The UK search campaign isn't spending due to budget restrictions, with target ROAS set at 3x. Given UK's poor performance across the board, the constraint may be protecting the client — but it also means UK Google has no active strategy.

-23% email revenue against +37% traffic is the most important contradiction in the audit. More people arriving, more joining the list — yet Klaviyo generates less revenue. Either new subscribers from paid are lower quality, campaign strategy has weakened, or segmentation is poor. Likely all three.
The automation stack is genuinely valuable. Billing reminders, failed payment recovery, and reactivation flows are all live and performing well. This infrastructure has compounding value and is the strongest argument in the agency's favour.
Welcome flow is critically underperforming. At €1.76 revenue per recipient, the most important automation for converting new subscribers is failing. Industry benchmark for subscription brands is €8–15 RPR. This needs a full rebuild focused on ingredient education, social proof, and subscription value communication.
Cancellation winback at €0.76/recipient is a missed opportunity. Given LTV reaches €250+ at month 10, winning back a churned subscriber is enormously valuable. A properly built winback flow should be a priority rebuild.

The bottom line

The €12,600/month fee is not justified by the results. Sisterly is paying full price for Meta, Google, email, and creative management — and getting flat revenue, a 1.63x Meta ROAS, and a Google account where Demand Gen is actively destroying value at 0.45x ROAS.

The agency deserves credit for the email automation infrastructure and shows genuine capability on IE Meta. But the structural problems — UK spend without a working strategy, Demand Gen at sub-0.5x ROAS, and a -23% email revenue decline against +37% traffic — are not the results of a team actively optimising the account.

Meta ROAS continues to decline. 1.57x in May vs 1.63x for the full Jan–Apr period. The ASC campaign (PROS_ASC_PURCH_PROSPA) is absorbing €4,750 of the €8,654 budget — over 54% of Meta spend — at a 1.32x ROAS and €86 CPA. This one campaign is the primary drag on Meta performance.
Google Demand Gen still running despite the evidence. Three Demand Gen campaigns (IRE TOF, IRE retargeting, UK image ads) spent a combined €627 in May at sub-1x ROAS across two of the three. The UK Demand Gen at 0.83x and €180 CPA continues the same pattern seen in the Jan–Apr data. These should have been paused.
Email is the bright spot in May. €12,214 attributed revenue with a +20.5% improvement vs the prior period is a meaningful positive shift — the first time email has shown growth in the audit data. Flows now represent 58% of email revenue (€7,120), which is a healthy balance. This trend needs to hold through June to confirm a recovery.
Google brand search performing well. IRE brand search at 25.49x ROAS and UK brand search at 4.30x ROAS are doing their job. Sisterlyed IRE Shopping campaign at 13.40x continues to be the best-value campaign in the Google account with minimal spend.
UK Meta is showing mixed signals. The UK Evergreen campaign achieved 2.33x ROAS at €54 CPA in May — the best UK performance seen in the entire audit. However with only 16 purchases and 21 days of data, this is too small a sample to draw firm conclusions. Worth monitoring closely in June.
